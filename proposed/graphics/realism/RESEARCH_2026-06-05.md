## 1. 🌍 ATMOSPHERIC SCATTERING

### `@takram/three-atmosphere` — **Best-in-Class for Three.js**
📦 **NPM:** https://www.npmjs.com/package/@takram/three-atmosphere
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/takram-design-engineering/three-geospatial


A Three.js and R3F (React Three Fiber) implementation of Eric Bruneton's Precomputed Atmospheric Scattering.
 
This library is part of a project to prototype the rendering aspect of a Web GIS engine.


**Key capabilities:**
- 
Suitable for large-scale scenes, but supports only Lambertian BRDF.

- 
Currently developed using GLSL.

## 2. ☀️ GOD RAYS / VOLUMETRIC LIGHT SHAFTS

### `Erkaman/glsl-godrays` — **Pure GLSL Module (Recommended)**
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/Erkaman/glsl-godrays
🎮 **Live Demo:** http://erkaman.github.io/glsl-godrays/


The implementation uses three rendering passes: Pass 1 renders all occluding geometry as black and the light source normally to an "occlusion texture" (which can be smaller than screen size for performance). Pass 2 renders everything normally. Pass 3 uses alpha blending to combine volumetric light rays in a fullscreen pass over the scene.


### Three.js Demos — Fake Volumetric Approach
🔗 **Demo:** https://threejsdemos.com/demos/lighting/godrays


Simulates god rays using a translucent cone mesh with additive blending. ConeGeometry creates light shaft geometry from point light source. Additive blending means overlapping rays add together for bright concentration effects. Depth write is disabled to prevent the cone from blocking other transparent objects.


### Medium Tutorial: GPU Gems 3 Implementation
🔗 **Article:** https://medium.com/@andrew_b_berg/volumetric-light-scattering-in-three-js-6e1850680a41


Based on Kenny Mitchell's Volumetric Light Scattering as a Post-Process from GPU Gems 3. The general technique renders the light source as white and all occluding objects as black to a texture, applies a parameterized radial blur with a shader, then additively blends over the normally lit scene.
 Key uniform controls: 
exposure controls overall intensity, decay controls fall off from the light source, density controls separation between samples, and weight provides fine-grained brightness control. Each becomes a uniform in the shader.


### Path-Tracing Approach (Advanced)
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/erichlof/THREE.js-PathTracing-Renderer


Includes a Light Shaft Demo demonstrating volumetric light shafts ('God rays') which occur when light sources are blocked everywhere except for the lit volume.

## 4. 💧 WATER CAUSTICS

### Evan Wallace — WebGL Water (Seminal Demo)
🔗 **Article:** https://medium.com/@evanwallace/rendering-realtime-caustics-in-webgl-2a99a29a0b2c
🎮 **Demo:** http://madebyevan.com/webgl-water/


The vertex shader positions vertices by tracing a ray along the sunlight direction, refracting it through the surface, and intersecting it with ground geometry. The same technique can be used for computing caustics due to reflection off the top of the water's surface.
 
An increase in triangle area means light is spread out and must be dimmed; a decrease means light is focused and should be brighter. The brightness change is proportional to the ratio of original to final area, avoiding the need for massive sampling.


### Martin Renou — Generalized Caustics with Three.js
🔗 **Article:** https://medium.com/@martinRenou/real-time-rendering-of-water-caustics-59cda1d74aa


To get stable 60fps, caustics must be computed entirely using GLSL shaders on the GPU. The approach: compute refracted rays at the water surface, compute where rays hit the environment, then compute intensity by checking where rays converge.


### NVIDIA GPU Gems — Rendering Water Caustics (Academic Reference)
🔗 **Chapter:** https://developer.nvidia.com/gpugems/gpugems/part-i-natural-effects/chapter-2-rendering-water-caustics


Calculating caustics per-pixel instead of per-vertex improves overall visual quality and decouples the effect from geometric complexity. Both GPU approaches use partial derivatives of the wave function to generate a normal.


### `pabennett/WaterCaustics` — OpenGL Experiments
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/pabennett/WaterCaustics


Caustic generation is performed by tracing the path of refracted light rays through the water's surface and calculating the interception point on the sea bed.
 
Three water surface simulation methods: FBO ping-pong for shallow water ripples, Sombrero function, and Tessendorf's FFT synthesis technique.

## 5. 🔲 SCREEN SPACE AMBIENT OCCLUSION (SSAO)

### N8AO — **Best Third-Party AO for Three.js (Recommended)**
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/N8python/n8ao


An efficient and visually pleasing implementation of SSAO with an emphasis on temporal stability and artist control. N8AOPass works with logarithmic depth buffers and orthographic cameras, supports materials with custom vertex displacement and alpha clipping, and automatically detects these features.


**Setup is minimal:**

Simply create an EffectComposer, add N8AOPass (which replaces RenderPass), and the effect works out of the box. Use of SMAAPass is recommended as hardware antialiasing does NOT work with ambient occlusion.


**Key parameters:** 
aoRadius controls the radius/size in world units. Set it too low and AO becomes an edge detector; too high and it becomes "soft." The radius should be one or two magnitudes less than scene scale.


### Comprehensive AO Pass (Multiple Algorithms)
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/Rabbid76/three-js-ao-pass


Implements SAO, N8AO, HBAO, and GTAO algorithms
 with references to all academic papers.

## 6. 🌑 SHADOW MAPPING

### Three.js Built-in Shadow System

Shadow mapping creates realistic shadows in Three.js by rendering the scene twice: first from the light's perspective to create a depth map, then from the camera's view using this map to determine shadowed areas.


Shadow types available: `BasicShadowMap`, `PCFShadowMap`, `PCFSoftShadowMap`, `VSMShadowMap`.

**Official examples:**
- Standard: https://threejs.org/examples/webgl_shadowmap.html
- VSM: https://threejs.org/examples/webgl_shadowmap_vsm.html
- CSM: https://threejs.org/examples/webgl_shadowmap_csm.html

### `three-csm` — **Cascaded Shadow Maps (Critical for Vanguard1's Tactical Map)**
🔗 **GitHub:** https://github.com/StrandedKitty/three-csm
📦 **NPM:** https://www.npmjs.com/package/three-csm


This approach provides higher resolution of shadows near the camera and lower resolution far away by using several shadow maps. CSMs are usually used for shadows cast by the sun over a large terrain.



The split mode defines how the frustum is split into smaller ones. Can be uniform (linear), logarithmic, practical, or custom. For most cases practical may be the best choice.

## 8. 🌫️ ATMOSPHERIC HAZE & DISTANCE FOG

### WebGL Fundamentals — Fog Tutorial (Definitive Reference)
🔗 **Tutorial:** https://webglfundamentals.org/webgl/lessons/webgl-fog.html


What you do is use a depth/distance calculation in shaders to make the color more or less the fog color: `gl_FragColor = mix(originalColor, fogColor, fogAmount);`


**Three fog types:**
1. **Linear:** Use `smoothstep(fogNear, fogFar, fogDistance)`
2. **Exponential:** 
Fog works exponentially, getting thicker with the square of the distance from the viewer.

3. **Height-based:** Use the `jwagner/terrain` atmospheric fog shader for realistic results.

### Height-Based Atmospheric Fog (Inigo Quilez Technique)
🔗 **GitHub (GLSL):** https://github.com/jwagner/terrain/blob/master/shaders/atmosphere.glsl

Based on http://www.iquilezles.org/www/articles/fog/fog.htm — combines exponential density with vertical falloff for realistic atmospheric haze.

### Dev.to — Dunes Terrain Fog (Practical WebGL Example)
🔗 **Article:** https://dev.to/keaukraine/rendering-dunes-terrain-in-webgl-30k2


Uses simple linear fog calculated in the vertex shader for performance. Fog range is controlled by two uniforms — fogStartDistance and fogDistance. Fragment shader applies fog color, which is adjusted for far terrain edges to blend with sky texture.
